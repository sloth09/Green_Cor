4. KEY FINDINGS & RECOMMENDATIONS
================================================================================

OPTIMAL SOLUTION: Case 2-2 (Ulsan Route)
  • Total NPC (20 years): $94.9 Million
  • Cost Advantage: 38.2% less than Busan storage scenario
  • Shuttle Configuration: 5,000 m3 vessels
  • Pump Capacity: 2,000 m3/h
  • Route Distance: 25 nautical miles (vs 86 nm for Yeosu, intra-port for Busan)
  • Initial Fleet: 8 shuttles (2030)

CASE COMPARISON:
  Case 1 (Busan Storage):    $153.5M NPC
  Case 2-1 (Yeosu Route):    $158.2M NPC (+3.1%)
  Case 2-2 (Ulsan Route):    $94.9M NPC  (BEST - Reference)

CRITICAL FACTORS:
  1. Storage tank CAPEX ($28.9M) significantly increases Case 1 cost
  2. Route distance directly impacts fuel consumption (33% reduction from Yeosu to Ulsan)
  3. Equipment sizing (5,000 m3 optimal for long-distance routes) affects efficiency
  4. Phased deployment strategy minimizes stranded assets (6-8 shuttles initially, 10% annual growth)

STRATEGIC RECOMMENDATIONS:
  1. Prioritize Ulsan Route Development (38% cost savings)
  2. Standardize Equipment (5,000 m3 shuttles, 2,000 m3/h pumps for long-distance)
  3. Reassess Storage Investment (high CAPEX not justified unless geographically critical)
  4. Implement Phased Deployment (start 2030, reach 500 vessels by 2050)

5. METHODOLOGY
================================================================================

OPTIMIZATION MODEL: Mixed-Integer Linear Programming (MILP)
  • Objective: Minimize Net Present Cost across 2030-2050
  • Planning Horizon: 20 years with 7% discount rate
  • Decision Variables: Fleet size, equipment specs, annual bunkering cycles
  • Cost Components: CAPEX (vessels, equipment, storage) + OPEX (fuel, maintenance, cooling)
  • Solver: CBC (Coin-or Branch and Cut) via PuLP with <0.1% optimality gap
  • Total Solutions Evaluated: 270 feasible scenarios (90 per case)
